Campus Safety Resources

Personal Safety Tips

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts
  • Travel in groups when possible, especially at night
  • Keep your personal belongings secure and within sight
  • Program emergency contacts into your phone for quick access
  • Share your class and activity schedule with trusted friends or family
  • Use well-lit and populated paths when walking at night
  • Report suspicious activities or individuals to campus security

Active Threat Response

If you encounter an active threat situation, remember these key actions:

Run

  • If there is an accessible escape path, evacuate the premises
  • Leave your belongings behind
  • Help others escape if possible
  • Prevent others from entering the area
  • Call 911 when you are safe

Hide

  • If evacuation is not possible, find a place to hide
  • Lock and barricade doors
  • Silence your cell phone
  • Hide behind large objects
  • Remain quiet and calm

Fight

  • As a last resort, and only when your life is in imminent danger
  • Attempt to incapacitate the threat
  • Act with physical aggression
  • Improvise weapons
  • Commit to your actions

Fire Emergency

  1. If you discover a fire, activate the nearest fire alarm pull station
  2. Call 911 and campus security at 555-123-4567
  3. Evacuate the building using the nearest exit
  4. Do not use elevators
  5. If trapped, close doors between you and the fire
  6. Signal for help from windows if possible
  7. Proceed to the designated assembly area for your building
  8. Do not re-enter the building until authorized by emergency personnel

Medical Emergency

  1. Call 911 for serious or life-threatening conditions
  2. Contact campus security at 555-123-4567
  3. Send someone to meet emergency responders and guide them to the location
  4. Do not move the injured/ill person unless they are in immediate danger
  5. If trained, provide appropriate first aid until help arrives
  6. For non-emergency medical assistance, contact the campus health center at 555-123-7890

Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) are located in all major campus buildings, typically near main entrances or elevators.

Severe Weather

Tornado

  • Move to the lowest level of the building
  • Stay away from windows and exterior doors
  • Seek shelter in interior hallways, stairwells, or rooms
  • Crouch down and cover your head and neck
  • Wait for the all-clear notification

Flooding

  • Avoid walking or driving through flood waters
  • Move to higher ground if outside
  • If indoors, move to higher floors
  • Report flooding to campus security
